# Demo 1 — Pane alignment

The first of plggmatic's three pillars is the
**column-oriented pane alignment system**. This demo
proves it with the raw layout combinators —
`row`, `column`, and the landmark panes
(`navPane`/`mainPane`/`asidePane`) — composed by hand,
not through the scheduler.

A screen is a `row` of `column` tracks; each column stacks
landmark **panes**; a track's sizing is a composed atom —
`basis("220px")` for a fixed width, `fluid` for the one
that fills the remaining space. There is no "hidden pane"
state: a pane you don't want is a pane you don't compose,
so collapsing one simply omits its column from the row and
the `fluid` main track reflows to fill the gap.

**[Run demo 1](/example/demo1.html)** — collapse the nav
or the aside, and cycle the nav track between a fixed
`basis` and `fluid`; watch the geometry and the real
`nav`/`main`/`aside` landmarks respond.

The [pane alignment](/pane-alignment) and
[multi-column renderer](/multi-column) pages explain the
system; the [workbench](/workbench) reaches the same
geometry through `schedule` + `multiColumn`. This demo
shows the primitives directly.
